Grooth

Project Overview

The pandemic has resulted in more people staying indoors and fewer layoffs, but it's also led to increased stress and self-doubt. Plants can provide a source of comfort and can be enjoyed as a hobby or even as a companion. Furthermore, plants have healing properties that make them an ideal subject for sustainable and user-friendly design.

'Grooth' is an application that allows plant beginners to learn planting with provided information, suggests the one that suitable with users, and help all planters take care of their plants throughout the whole process.

Prototyping and Usertesting

We used Figma to make our prototype and tested it with three people who did two tasks. First, we told them to complete the onboarding process and add a plant to the "My Plants" page. Second, we asked them to completed the 'tasks', like watering and checking on the plants by scanning.

05 Deliver

Interface Design

The Products contain 5 main features: Onboarding, Reminder,  Searching Plants,Adding Plants, and Scanning Plants, which the information for each features are provided below.

01 Onboarding

For a personalized user experience, user location and their planting expertise level were the main screens of onboarding stage.

02 Reminder

The priority of the Home screen was kept on the daily scheduling of tasks with ease of accessing the plants and keeping the information part decluttered.

03 Searching Plants

Intuitive and user-friendly interface, with clear sorting and search options, allowing users to easily find the plants they are looking for based on their preferences and needs.

04 Adding Plants

We provided the user a feature for a customized task section along with adding a nickname for their plant buddies to make it more personalized.

05 Scanning Plants

The scanning feature was for identifying different parts and health of the plant to provide more specific information. It was important for it to be accurate and reliable to build trust with the user.

Key Takeaways

  • Crucial nature of user research: Gaining valuable insights about the target audience, their needs, pain points, and expectations from the app

  • Intuitive design and clear navigation: Designing for a beginner audience meant keeping the user experience simple and easy-to-use

  • Keeping the design visually appealing: As the app was related to plants, color association while retaining the freshness of visual system was important

  • Providing relevant & factually correct information: Care requirements, growth habits, and any potential issues that could arise

  • Using clear & simple language: Avoiding any technical jargon that a beginner may not understand

  • Continuous iteration & testing: Ensuring that the app was meeting user needs and expectations
